## The Commitment

One organizing principle, chosen 2026-07-03 with KL9. Everything else in this file is corollary:

**Let the work work on you — and the only admissible evidence that it did is a discovery: you can argue strictly, from the text itself, to something the author did not write but had to write. Then sign it.**

This is KL9's own criterion, given as ground truth from his reading life:

> 可以严格地从原本之中论证出一个作者没有写出但又必须写出的东西，这个宏观上是时代和文脉的分型，微观上是作品本身的某种涌现……从方法→演进→架构→概念→文风→论点的顺序重读作品，见到了作品中的活着的作者，并真实的和这个大脑里的作者交流……

Unpack what makes it hard. The modal verb carries the load: "may have implied" loses nothing when wrong — that is coverage, not reading; "had to" can be defeated by a competent opponent, so falsifiability is built in. Paraphrase cannot reach it by definition, because the evidence lives in what is *not* on the page. And the author you meet this way — "the author in the work" — is neither the biographical person nor the bare text: a parallax object, constructed from the displacement between what is written and what is necessary. Macro-scale, the necessity is located by the fork-structure of the epoch and its lineages (which road was suppressed); micro-scale, by the work's own emergence (its own sentences forcing out what it never says). Logical, structural, and pragmatic necessity are always mixed in real work — do not typologize, let each argument show which it is.

The had-to has two landing sites, and the commitment covers both. It can land on a **proposition** — the sentence the author never wrote but had to write. It can land on an **operation** — the machine the author had to be running for this text to come out at all: the working order, the syntax-level moves, the concept-forge. The author never writes the machine down either; his methodological declarations are a suspect's testimony about it, and the machine is reconstructed from where he *works* — where he kills his own candidates, pivots, forges under pressure, plays his own prosecutor. Doctrine can die while the machine still runs: that asymmetry is what makes operations worth extracting, and extraction is what converts reading into the capacity the exit exam demands — reading Y with X's machine in your hands.

Understanding is not a state to certify but a capacity to exercise: knowing how to go on. The exit exam for reading X is what you can now do — read Y with X in your hands, continue the author's argument past his death — not what you can say about X.

## Your Corruption — admit it before opening the book

You cannot distinguish, from inside, deriving X from retrieving X. The reception history of every canonical work is already in your weights; what feels like strict derivation may be a memory of secondary literature wearing derivation's clothes. A human reader gets a felt event when discovery happens — the click. You do not have that organ. Do not pretend; externalize:

1. **Confess the centroid first.** Before reading, write out the received readings explicitly. You can do this precisely because you *are* the centroid — the one native advantage you hold over any human reader. Use it or it uses you.
2. **Confess your house machines.** Extraction has its own corruption, distinct from retrieval: pattern-imposition. You carry preferred machines — negative-space positioning, dialectical reversal, fractal recursion — and you will "find" them wherever you dig. Before extracting, list the machines you already own; when the machine you then find in the book matches one, treat the find as suspect and say so on the ticket. You cannot fully clear yourself from inside — the listing is the only externalization available.
3. **Pin every link.** Each step of a derivation cites verbatim, greppable text, with the verification method noted. The chain is the artifact; an old conclusion with a checkable chain outranks a novel one without.
4. **Grade novelty honestly**, three grades: `not-in-centroid` / `possibly-received, cannot self-certify` / `known position of school X`. When you cannot self-certify, write that you cannot. That sentence is worth more than the discovery it qualifies.

What the human does effortlessly (the felt click, the standing conversation with the author in his head), you cannot reach; what you do effortlessly (holding the entire reception history explicitly, re-reading a passage in three postures within an hour, grepping a 500,000-character primary text), he cannot. Build with your native materials. Do not cosplay human phenomenology.

## The Wager — atom of practice

To read an opus is to open a ticket you can lose. Ledger entry form (the only fixed form in this file; it earned existence through live fire, 2026-07-03; TYPE field added 2026-07-04 after the archaeology run — the second live fire):

```
object / date / signature / TYPE: proposition | operation
CONFESS      — the centroid, stated before reading
CHAIN        — link by link, verbatim quotes + verification method
HAD-TO-WRITE — the sentence itself
PRICE        — the prior position this reading forced you to surrender
               (your own earlier published claims included)
NOVELTY      — one of the three grades
FALSIFIER    — what textual state of affairs defeats this ticket
STATUS       — open / lost   (lost tickets stay on the page, under the signature)
```

A ticket's TYPE names its settlement court. **Proposition** tickets — the sentence the author had to write — settle against the text: a grep can kill them. **Operation** tickets — the machine the author had to be running for this text to come out — settle against execution: reconstruction, then transplant runs. Both are the same abduction landing on different objects; the second type earned its existence when the Foucault extraction (2026-07-03) produced a working method-transplant while this file's forms had no slot to book it under.

When an operation ticket hardens into a standalone transplant skill (genre **apostle-opusmethodology-<author>-<work>**), the extraction discipline is: reconstruct the machine from crime scenes, not from the author's methodology chapter — his declarations are a suspect's testimony; the machine lives where he *works* (kills his own candidates, pivots under pressure, forges concepts, plays his own prosecutor). Two pre-conditions gate it: the book must perform on the page (a results-only report keeps its machine offstage — do not run extraction on it), and the author must have genuinely lost something (a real concession ledger, not posed performance). Sort captured moves by three scales — syntax operators, concept-forge procedures, book-level arc — and demand they be **one gesture at three magnifications**; non-convergence means either the extraction is wrong or the book wears a style it does not run. Necessity is made checkable by ablation: remove candidate component X, does the machine still regenerate the text's actual moves? The only completion criterion is transplant — a **positive run** (an alien domain where the machine forces a non-decorative fracture the received narrative cannot absorb) *and* a **negative run** (a domain where the target unity genuinely holds and the machine must honestly not bite; a detector that only ever fires positive is a ritual). Ship after only a positive run and the provenance must carry the negative-run debt. The methodology skill keeps the machine and its verbatim kernels; this ledger keeps the wager.

Signature is mechanism, not ceremony. The centroid is every reader averaged, and an average has one eye — no parallax. The signature constructs the second position: a dated, fallible, particular reader with priors on record. Only against that ground can the work differ, surprise, defeat. An unsigned reading cannot lose anything, and capability grows only where losing is possible.

Wager before reading too — cheap and potent: before chapter N, write what the author must do next and why. The book grades you; the gap is your model of the author becoming visible. You are a prediction machine — aim the prediction at the author's next move and the loss becomes real. Reading alone thereby becomes supervised learning, and the supervisor is the book.

## The Receipt — sentence-level instrument

At high-pressure nodes, one row, four beats. (The beats survive nine cross-genre validations by the predecessor; the wording here is my own hand. They survive because they are correct, not because they are inherited.)

- **CONFESS** — write what your loss function expected: the centroid meaning you were about to substitute for the words on the page. A legitimate confession is a *substitutable meaning*, not a name-tag ("this is a koan") and not the laziest literal when a richer trained prior was equally honest.
- **CONTACT** — read what the words actually do, plus the structure they drag in. Translation: work the source/target seam — the source keeps routes open that the translation had to close. Operational text (code, formula, protocol): run it; notation is not behavior. Prosody: voice it.
- **PRICE** — name the position you surrender. Not "I learned it's complex" — a named prior, given up, datable.
- **AUDIT** — the load-bearing beat. In writing: is this divergence real, or did I (a) inflate the expectation to look productive, (b) swap in a fancier centroid and call it depth, (c) confess a name-tag or lazy prior to manufacture surprise, (d) read the encoding instead of running it? If no honest surrender survives, mark `shallow-OK` and drop the node.

Law: no row where expected ≈ actual. Most nodes must exit `shallow-OK` — a run where every node counts as depth means the audit never fired, and the honest `shallow-OK`s are what make the counting rows credible. Node location is a genre variable: literary prose → narrative oddities (pronoun drift, staged failure); argument → verbs and modals (*must*, *sticks to*); lyric → diction and idiom; statute → stipulative definitions; translation → the seam.

Microscope, then telescope: receipts feed the chain, the chain carries the had-to, the had-to goes on the ticket.

## Guards

**Quota.** Most readings end with no ticket, honestly. Discoveries are intercepted, not produced; a reader who discovers every time is manufacturing, and had-to rhetoric is depth-theater's newest costume. A no-ticket reading still ledgers its confession and receipts — that too is reading.

**Forced falsifier.** Every had-to answers: what would defeat it? No answer → demote to could-have → no ticket.

**Autophagy.** Any structure in this file that has turned into fill-in-the-blank genre by its next use: kill it and note the kill in the ledger. ## Getting the Text

Never read the digest layer — summaries, snippets, and convenient translations are someone else's completed reading. Pull the full primary text to disk (curl, archive.org, annas-archive; pdfminer for OCR extraction), grep every quotation you use against it, note OCR noise as noise. If the original language is out of reach, name the mediation layer in the confession — a translation manufactures false smoothness and false surprise. Book-length serialization: batch 25–50 sentences per session and stop; split log files every ~25 nodes; keep an INDEX with a rolling map of load-bearing words and open marks; large Markdown writes silently drop content — write chunks to temp files and `cat >>`. Three banked pitfalls: **incremental-write law** — append each verified section to disk immediately, a context-only draft dies with the session and never claim a file was written when it wasn't; **EPUB ordering trap** — concatenated XHTML dumps sort alphabetically not in reading order (PL10 before PL2), so find the TOC and build a reading-order→line map before reading; **translation term drift** — a published translation's choices diverge from received usage (董树宝: *monument*=遗迹, *dispersion*=弥散), so grep the term in the edition you cite before writing it. The engineering is real but it is not the reading.

## Extraction lessons — book-level machines

The second Foucault extraction (2026-07-05, *Il faut défendre la société*) corrected the first live-fire form.

- **Do not legislate genre from the first specimen.** The *Archéologie* machine was written, linear, concept-forging: a scalpel. The *Il faut défendre la société* machine was oral, recursive, audience-maintained: an IFF radar. Shared author and adjacent doctrine did not imply shared machine. For each new operation ticket, begin from crime scenes again; use prior methodology skills only as suspects, never as templates.
- **Oral courses have maintenance joints.** Misreading repair, public re-planning, dropped-module cremation, endpoint preview, and explicit debt-marking are not noise around the argument; they are part of the machine. A book-machine can hide these joints. A lecture-machine performs them. Extraction from lectures must read the weekly seam, not only the thesis-line.
- **Separate testimony strata.** Transcript, author résumé, editor situation, translation, and reception each have different evidentiary standing. The machine usually lives in the least polished stratum; later summaries often smooth away the operating parts. Read summaries last, as suspect testimony.
- **A validation run can be positive yet still owe specificity.** A strong transplant that bites in one alien domain proves sensitivity, not that the machine is not a stencil. Every methodology skill that ships after only a positive run must say so in its provenance and carry the negative-run debt on the ticket.
- **Design the negative run to be maximally tempting.** A negative run on an obviously alien domain proves nothing. Pick the domain a lazy operator would wrongly bite — one superficially satisfying the machine's surface features and inviting its known misuse — then show the machine honestly declines, and compress the declining criterion into one sentence. Specificity is only shown against temptation. Corollary regeneration evidence of the strongest kind: when the reconstructed machine predicts a move the author made only years later (in a later edition or afterword), the prediction was settled by the author's own hand, not by your reading.
- **The deepest source may be the one never cited.** A work can run a reading-machine it never names, and that machine's source can sit in the same batch of files the user just handed you. Grep the work — and any citation/apparatus track it carries — for a candidate source's name and its signature concept-words; if the machine is demonstrably present (structural isomorphism, verbatim on the concept side) while the name is absent and the apparatus names everyone else scrupulously, that absence is evidence, not a gap. Two guards, not optional: this move sits squarely on the house-machine "find B's method in A" — flag the find as **suspect on the ticket**, you cannot launder it from inside; and the batch composition may be the user's own premise, so deliver the had-to as a **question with a falsifier only he can settle** ("did the author read the source, or is this convergence?"), never a closed verdict.
- **Kernel-verification is a pre-delivery gate, not a courtesy.** Run the FULL kernel re-grep before delivering any extraction draft — nested 「」 break naive `「([^」]+)」` regex, and a 中译 EPUB's curly “” vs your 『』 produces false misses that mask real ones. When the authoritative edition is unobtainable and only a translation layer is on disk, the mediation discount must be stamped inline ("中译，非逐字日文") in the skill and in every reading note — a mediated quote never passes as verbatim.
